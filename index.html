Riding the Iconic Cable Cars

Few experiences capture the spirit of the bay quite like a ride on its legendary cable cars. Since 1873, these rolling landmarks have carried millions of passengers up and down the city's steep hills, offering breathtaking views and a healthy dose of nostalgia along the way.

The three surviving lines — Powell-Hyde, Powell-Mason, and California Street — each offer a unique perspective on the city. Whether you're clinging to the running board over Russian Hill or gliding past the Financial District, the cable car remains the most charming way to travel.

If you're planning to ride, aim for early mornings on weekdays to avoid the longest queues. A single ride costs $8, but day passes and multi-ride tickets can save you money if you intend to hop on more than once. And don't forget — the turnaround at Fisherman's Wharf is worth the visit on its own for the street performers and fresh seafood stands nearby.

For the best photographic opportunities, ride the Powell-Hyde line at sunset. The golden light spilling over Alcatraz and the Marin Headlands creates a backdrop that no filter can improve.

Cable Car Tips

Best time to ride: Early mornings on weekdays (before 9 AM) have the shortest wait times. Weekends and holidays can mean hour-long queues at popular stops.

Tickets: A one-way ride is $8 per person. Purchase a Visitor Passport ($24/day) for unlimited rides on all Muni transit, including cable cars.

Safety first: Keep hands and arms inside the car at all times. If you're standing on the running board, hold on tightly — especially on downhill stretches.

Accessibility: Cable cars are not wheelchair accessible. Muni buses run parallel routes and offer full accessibility.

Fun fact: The cable car system is the last manually operated cable car system in the world and is designated a National Historic Landmark.
